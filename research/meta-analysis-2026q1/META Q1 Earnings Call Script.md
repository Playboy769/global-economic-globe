# Meta Platforms, Inc. (META) Q1 FY2026 Earnings Call Transcript

Powered by Quartr · Apr 29, 2026, 5:30 PM EDT

## Summary
Meta delivered Q1 2026 revenue of $56.3B, up 33% YoY, with operating income of $22.9B (41% margin) and strong growth in ad revenue and engagement. Management raised CapEx guidance for the year due to higher component costs and reaffirmed its commitment to aggressive AI and infrastructure investment, while maintaining expense guidance and signaling ongoing efficiency initiatives.

## Participants
- Kenneth Dorell — Director of Investor Relations
- Mark Zuckerberg — CEO
- Susan Li — CFO

## Key figures (from prepared remarks)
- Family DAP: 3.56B in March (slight QoQ decline from Dec due to Iran internet disruptions + Russia WhatsApp restriction; absent these, FoA DAP would have grown QoQ)
- Total FoA revenue: $55.9B, +33% YoY
- FoA ad revenue: $55B, +33% (+29% cc)
- Ad impressions: +19%; average price per ad: +12% YoY
- FoA other revenue: $885M, +74% (WhatsApp paid messaging + subscriptions)
- Reality Labs revenue: $402M, -2% YoY (lower Quest headset sales, offset by AI glasses growth)
- Total revenue: $56.3B, +33% (+29% cc)
- Total expenses: $33.4B, +35% YoY (infra + employee comp)
- Headcount: >77,900, -1% QoQ from Q4
- Operating income: $22.9B, 41% operating margin
- Interest and other income: -$1.1B (unrealized losses on equity investments)
- Tax rate: -23% (favorably impacted by $8.03B tax benefit partially relieving the $15.93B non-cash tax charge from Q3 2025 re: capitalized R&D; absent benefit, Q1 tax rate would be 14%)
- Net income: $26.8B; EPS: $10.44 (absent tax benefit: $18.7B / $7.31)
- CapEx (incl finance lease principal): $19.8B
- Free cash flow: $12.4B
- Cash & marketable securities: $81.2B; Debt: $58.7B
- Contractual commitments step-up: +$107B this quarter (multi-year cloud deals + infra purchase agreements)

## Engagement / recommendations metrics
- Instagram Reels: +10% time spent from Q1 ranking improvements
- Facebook total video time: +8% globally QoQ (largest QoQ gain in 4 years); US/Canada video watch time +9%
- Doubled length of user interaction sequences for IG training in Q1
- Same-day posts now >30% of recommended Reels on IG and FB (>2x vs a year ago)
- >0.5B users each on FB and IG watching AI-translated videos weekly
- Meta AI: double-digit % increase in sessions per user post Muse Spark rollout

## Monetization / ads
- Adaptive ranking model (LLM-scale, trillion parameters) expanded to offsite conversions: +1.6% conversion rate across major FB/IG surfaces
- Lattice + GEM architecture advances: +6% conversion rate for landing page view ads
- Meta AI business assistant fully rolled out; common account issues resolved 20% higher rate
- Meta Ads AI connectors in open beta this week
- >8M advertisers using gen AI ad creative tools; video generation +3% conversion rate
- Business AIs: >10M weekly conversations (up from 1M at start of year)
- Value optimization suite: annual revenue run rate >$20B (>2x YoY)
- Partnerships ads: run rate >$10B (>2x YoY)

## Guidance
- Q2 2026 revenue: $58B-$61B (FX ~2% tailwind)
- FY2026 total expenses: $162B-$169B (unchanged); operating income expected above 2025
- FY2026 CapEx (incl finance lease principal): $125B-$145B (raised from $115B-$135B), due to higher component pricing + additional data center costs
- Tax rate remaining quarters 2026: 13%-16%
- Plan to reduce employee base in May 2026 (leaner operating model)
- Ongoing EU/US regulatory scrutiny; youth-related trials scheduled this year, may result in material loss

## Product / strategy notes
- Muse family of models; first model Muse Spark; first release from Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL)
- Meta AI significantly upgraded; near top of app stores
- Building personal agents + business agents forming an ecosystem
- Business AIs weekly conversations +10x since start of year
- >1 GW of custom silicon with Broadcom; significant AMD chips complementing new NVIDIA systems ("Meta Compute" initiative for efficiency)
- AI glasses: daily users tripled YoY; released Ray-Ban Meta Optics (all-day wear); Oakley last year; Ray-Ban Meta displays with Meta Neural Band
- Biggest investor in VR; focused on making VR business sustainable

## Q&A

**Brian Nowak (Morgan Stanley)** — signposts for ROIC on Muse/infrastructure investment over next 12-24 months.
- Zuckerberg: Formula = build experiences to billions, monetize at scale. Milestones: (1) technical quality to enable great product, (2) product scaling, (3) monetization, (4) drive efficiency toward profitability. No precise month-over-month plan; sense of the "shape." Comfortable lab is on track to be leading; Muse Spark high quality; Meta AI world-class assistant. Tracking training runs, product scaling, pipeline excitement; will ramp monetization over time.

**Mark Shmulik (Bernstein)** — team focus: further training runs / specialization vs product launches. + (Susan) 2027 CapEx step-up dimensioning.
- Zuckerberg: Roadmap consistent — research team scaling models for business/personal agents (next set of more advanced models in training now, a continuous loop); product team now unlocked to build on own strong models (previously prototyping on older/other-company models). Iterate on intelligence + build/scale products; as PMF reached, build businesses + decrease costs. "How we've done everything over 20 years."
- Li: No specific 2027 CapEx outlook; dynamic planning. "We have continued to underestimate our Compute needs." Compute becoming more central; building with flexibility — can bring online more slowly or reduce future spend if not needed.

**Eric Sheridan (Goldman Sachs)** — agentic compute for consumers + enterprises; extending media engagement + commerce to agentic; SMB/enterprise opportunity.
- Li: Near term — deepening engagement, more personalized ad experiences, helping SMBs. Agentic capabilities enabling personal + business agents interacting → commerce ecosystem (further out). Focus on personal superintelligence / consumer agent; monetization via commission structures or premium offering. Business AIs >10M weekly conversations (up from 1M); currently free for most businesses; longer-term monetization model to come.

**Youssef Squali (Truist)** — EssilorLuxottica additional brands gating factors + successful 2026 units. + (Susan) 10% RIF: AI efficiency vs staying fit; employee needs vs top-line.
- Li: Optimal company size unknown; leveraging AI tools to increase productivity, accelerating engineer output; bias toward building more products. Significant infra investments; continuously evaluating structure. Glasses: strong growth; sales shifting from prior-gen Ray-Ban Meta to latest gen (better battery, higher-res video capture); strong interest in Ray-Ban Meta displays with Meta Neural Band (next-gen display glasses).

**Justin Post (Bank of America)** — Muse Spark took ~10 months; what unlock for new products; product cadence next 9 months.
- Zuckerberg: Field moving quickly; happy lab went fastest from standup to widely-accepted strong model — validation. Cadence tough to say (competitively sensitive + quality over dates). "There aren't that many [agents] that I would want to give to my mother" — quality bar matters more than launch week. Teams make meaningful progress day over day. Picture comes into focus over subsequent quarters.

**Ross Sandler (Barclays)** — new consumer apps (OpenClaw, Poke, Dreamer acquisition); how changing Meta AI / agentic strategy. + will lab stay consumer or go code-writing / recursive self-improvement.
- Zuckerberg: OpenClaw etc. give exciting glimpse but rough (need local install, terminal config) — addressable to hundreds of thousands / millions. Meta focus: polished, easy, infrastructure done, works — addressable to billions. Same for businesses (create websites/products, grow). Understand people's goals, work on them, check back. Willing to pay for premium / high-Compute versions. Self-improvement important: can't build leading product without leading models; models must improve themselves. "People conflate coding with self-improvement more than they should." Not primarily a dev-tools company; focused on all parts necessary for self-improvement in service of personal superintelligence.

**Ron Josey (Citigroup)** — personal agent opportunity: short/medium/long-term? + (Susan) how doubling user interaction sequence length drives usage; room to go.
- Zuckerberg: Short-term versions + massive upside from more intelligence/capabilities. Agents = product vehicle for delivering capability. This year key for establishing the vehicle. Model improvement goes on a very long time.
- Li: Lots of room to improve recommendations. Continue improving data infrastructure (train on more data, richer content description, larger model architecture, longer histories). More personalized/relevant; redesigning content retrieval to match full range of interests, tailoring topic diversity to breadth of interests. LLM-based "tune your algorithm" natural-language feedback features. Sequence length is one of many Q1 improvements; big roadmap ahead.

**Doug Anmuth (JPMorgan)** — step-up from smaller models to Muse Spark / LLMs in ads; key unlocks. + Manus strategic importance / deal status.
- Li: Manus — still working through details, no update. Ads: advancing architecture to leverage larger models. Historically didn't use large architectures (e.g. GEM) for inference (cost-prohibitive, strict latency — find right ad within milliseconds); used them to transfer knowledge to smaller runtime models. H2 last year: adaptive ranking model leverages LLM-scale (trillion parameters), co-designed system with underlying silicon for sub-second speed; intelligently routes requests to more Compute-intensive models when higher conversion probability → better performance + inference ROI.

**Ken Gawrelski (Wells Fargo)** — Muse Spark shopping/commerce depth; learnings from 2021-2022 commerce push; next-gen marketplace opportunity. + (Susan) visibility into core business growth trajectory (growing 2x industry pace despite large share).
- Zuckerberg: "Loftier answer." AI agents get better with fully optimized stack → need to build frontier models + agents + infrastructure top to bottom. Bet that individuals and the things they care about will be more important in future. Different from industry rhetoric about centralized AI doing all productive work. Vision: society progresses via individuals pursuing aspirations (curing diseases → finding the right shirt for my daughter). "I don't hear any other labs out there talking about how they're building an AI that's really good at shopping." Shopping/local/social context/personal health/visual understanding (glasses) all elements of personal superintelligence. Investment in Meta = bet on empowering individuals.
- Li: Q2 guide embeds range of macro outcomes + ongoing engagement + ads performance work. ROI-based budgeting process funds ads initiatives driving future-year growth; robust impact measurement; feel good about investment opportunities ahead.
