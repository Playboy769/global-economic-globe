## RPO / commercial backlog
Commercial RPO $627B (+99% YoY); total company RPO $633B; ~25% converts within 12 months (+39% YoY); weighted
average duration ~2.5yr including OpenAI Azure commitments. Commercial bookings +7% YoY ex-OpenAI; -4% to -6% CC

as backlog *duration* extended — a nuance management did not verbally reconcile).

## OpenAI relationship (equity-method investee; secondary-source synthesis, flagged lower confidence pending primary
text — see access limitation above)
- ~27% ownership of OpenAI on an as-converted basis, equity-method accounted.
- Total funding commitment $13B; $11.8B funded as of Mar 31, 2026.
- Nine-month net gains from OpenAI investment: $5.9B (vs a $2.7B **loss** in the prior-year nine-month period) —
  driven primarily by a dilution gain tied to an October 2025 recapitalization (i.e. this landed mostly in fiscal
  Q2 FY26, Oct–Dec 2025, not the Q3 quarter this report covers).
- IP license: previously exclusive, tied to an "AGI achievement" trigger; now **non-exclusive** with a hard
  **2032** expiration — OpenAI may now sell API access through other cloud providers.
- Revenue share: OpenAI continues paying Microsoft 20% of revenue through 2030, but now **capped**; the dollar cap
  itself was not disclosed on the call or found in secondary sources reviewed.

## Other
- IRS transfer-pricing dispute: $28.9B additional tax sought (2004–2013 periods) plus penalties/interest;
  unrecognized tax benefits $29.3B vs $27.4B PY. Management contests; considers existing accruals adequate.
- Additional data-center leases not yet commenced: $196.6B (Note: single-source figure from secondary aggregation,
  not independently cross-checked against a second source — treat as lower confidence / directional only).
- Form 4: Satya Nadella adopted a new Rule 10b5-1 trading plan on March 8, 2026 (during an open window, ahead of
  the Apr 29 print and outside any quiet period), running to Sept 4, 2026; first trade under the plan is not
  scheduled until Aug 31, 2026 (tied to a performance-stock-award vest), i.e. **no discretionary trading activity
  by the CEO is scheduled around this specific Q3 print**. Aggregate 90-day insider-selling figures reported by
  third-party trackers (~$2.2B) reflect routine RSU-vesting/tax-withholding sales under pre-existing plans, not
  activity timed to this earnings release.

## Post-earnings market reaction (context only, not used for any recommendation)
Reported EPS/revenue beat consensus ($4.27 vs ~$4.06 est.; $82.9B vs ~$81.4B est.), but shares fell ~4-5% on Apr 30
to a close in the ~$402-408 range, with commentary attributing the drop to the $190B CY2026 capex guide (vs ~$155B
consensus) and the ~$25B component-price-inflation carve-out. By mid-July 2026 shares traded in the ~$390s.
