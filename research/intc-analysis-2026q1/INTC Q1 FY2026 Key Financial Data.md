**Central finding of this filing period**: Intel's GAAP results show a **$4,070M restructuring charge**
that drives a **GAAP net loss of $(3,728)M attributable to Intel, GAAP diluted EPS $(0.73)** — neither
the charge, the GAAP net loss, nor the EPS figure is mentioned anywhere in the earnings call prepared
remarks or Q&A (which only discuss non-GAAP gross margin 41% and non-GAAP EPS $0.29). This is not a
minor non-GAAP adjustment; it is the dominant line item in the entire income statement and was
disclosed only in the 10-Q filed the next day, not on the call itself.

## Fab 34 Ireland Buyout / Capital Structure Context (from call)

Intel closed the repurchase of the 49% equity interest in the Fab 34 (Ireland) joint investment,
funded with ~$7.7B cash and ~$6.5B new debt. Management guides resulting non-controlling-interest
(NCI) income/expense to net to ~$250M in each of Q2/Q3/Q4 FY2026 and ~$1.1B for FY2027 and FY2028
(GAAP basis). Debt maturities: committed to retiring $2.5B in FY2026 and $3.8B in FY2027. Note: the
10-Q balance sheet above (as of 2026-03-28) predates the "recently closed" Fab 34 transaction
referenced on the 2026-04-23 call — the associated debt raise and cash outlay are not yet reflected
in the Q1 FY26 balance sheet and should show up in the Q2 FY26 10-Q instead.

## Legal Proceedings (10-Q Note 14 / Item 1)

- **EC competition matter**: $310M accrued (incl. FX/interest) on a reduced €237M (~$277M) fine
  related to a "payments to prevent rival sales" finding; Intel appealed the General Court's December
  2025 reduction of the fine further to the Court of Justice in February 2026.
- **VLSI Technology patent litigation**: $1.0B accrued. In April 2026 the Federal Circuit reversed and
  remanded the original patent-infringement judgment back to district court; separately, new suits
  were filed in W.D. Texas covering 8 additional NXP-derived patents.
- **Stockholder derivative suit (new, filed March 2026, Delaware Chancery Court, removed to Delaware
  federal court April 2026)**: filed against Intel's directors, the U.S. Department of Commerce, and
  Secretary Howard Lutnick, challenging the August 22, 2025 Warrant and Common Stock Agreement between
  Intel and the DOC (the mechanism behind the U.S. government's equity/warrant stake in Intel) as an
  alleged breach of fiduciary duty.
- Other ongoing matters: consumer class actions (Spectre/Meltdown/Downfall), a securities class action
  tied to the 2024 segment-reporting/foundry-model restatement (dismissed, on appeal), and EireOg
  patent-indemnification suits on behalf of Dell/Cisco/IBM/Lenovo/HPE/AWS/Oracle, with trial dates
  scattered April–December 2026.

## 10-K (FY2025) — Supply Chain, Manufacturing Footprint, Customer Concentration

**Supply chain (Item 1, "Supply Chain")**: Intel discloses reliance on "thousands of suppliers
worldwide" but flags sole-source/concentration risk explicitly for (1) EUV lithography tools,
manufactured solely by **ASML**, required for leading-edge process technology, and (2) certain rare
earth minerals critical to a range of technology products, for which **China is the primary source of
global supply**.

**Manufacturing footprint (Item 1, "Manufacturing")**: 2025 key production fabs — Oregon (R&D +
ramping Intel 18A), Arizona (Intel 7 + ramping Intel 18A), Ireland (Intel 4 and Intel 3), Israel
(Intel 7). Key assembly/test sites — China, New Mexico (key advanced-packaging site), Vietnam,
Malaysia (new advanced-packaging facility under construction, matches the call's mention of a backend
Malaysia expansion). 2025 restructuring: consolidating Costa Rica assembly/test into Vietnam/Malaysia
(target complete end-2026); slowed Ohio fab construction; discontinued planned Germany fab and Poland
assembly/test expansions. No named third-party OSAT (outsourced assembly/test) partner is disclosed —
Intel describes assembly/test as internally operated.

**External foundry relationships**: **TSMC** is named explicitly as the key third-party foundry used
for select tiles or entire products "where advantageous for cost, performance, schedule," and Intel
states it expects to continue using TSMC and other third-party foundries. **UMC**: a 2024-announced
strategic collaboration on a 12nm process platform targeting production start in 2027. Intel 14A
strategy is explicitly conditioned on external customer wins — the 10-K states Intel "may pause or
discontinue" pursuit of next-generation leading-edge process technology "if we are unable to secure a
significant external foundry customer for Intel 14A" (echoed in Item 1A risk factors).
