## Guidance (from the press release)

**Q2 FY2026 (GAAP)**: subscription revenue $3,815–3,820M (+22.5% YoY); cRPO +19% (non-GAAP CC +19.5%);
operating margin 26.5% (includes ~125bp Armis headwind); diluted shares 1.04B.

**FY2026 (GAAP, raised at Q1)**: subscription revenue $15,735–15,775M (+22–22.5% YoY GAAP,
+20.5–21% CC non-GAAP) — raised $205M at the midpoint from prior guide, including ~125bp Armis
contribution; subscription gross margin 81.5% (incl. 25bp Armis headwind); operating margin 31.5%
(incl. 75bp Armis headwind); free cash flow margin 35% (incl. 200bp Armis headwind); diluted shares 1.04B.

Management states Armis margin headwinds are expected to fully normalize to zero by year-end FY2026
via Now-on-Now AI efficiencies, with operating/FCF margin expansion trajectories resuming in FY2027.

Q1 FY26 subscription growth absorbed a ~75bp headwind from delayed on-premise deal closings in the
Middle East (Iran conflict-related); FY2026 guidance reflects a "prudent" continued assessment of that
geopolitical risk.

- **2026-04-29**: Broad annual-cycle option grants to McDermott (346,920 options, $697.76 strike),
  Mastantuono (39,155 options, $655.94 strike) and Canney (19,575 options, $655.94 strike) — routine
  equity-refresh timing, not a reaction to the print.
- **2026-05-15**: Broad option exercises across McDermott, Mastantuono, Fipps, Canney, Zavery, Nowbar
  and Fontaine — consistent with scheduled vesting/exercise activity rather than a single coordinated signal.

No Form 4 shows insider selling concentrated in the days immediately before the April 22 call (which
would be a stronger red flag); the closest post-print transaction (Canney, 4/24) is small and routine.
The pre-print open-market purchase by the CEO (2/27) is, if anything, a mildly confidence-supportive
data point, though it is 7+ weeks removed from the print and should not be overweighted.
