## Core (non-GAAP) Reconciliation

- Core net sales: $4,345M (+18% YoY); core net income: $612M (+31%); core diluted EPS: $0.70 (vs $0.54, +30%)
- Core net income ($612M) exceeds GAAP net income attributable to Corning ($371M) by ~$241M. Reconciling items include: translated earnings contract loss ($16M), translation gain on FX debt ($6M), unallocated R&D (~$43M), amortization of intangibles ($23M), interest expense net (~$61M), an income tax benefit (~$91M) excluded from core, and restructuring/other charges (~$44M).
- Note: the stated definition of core-vs-GAAP differences (per the call's IR intro) explicitly includes "discrete tax items and other tax-related adjustments" — this is very likely where the $92M South Korea noncash tax charge (see below) gets excluded from core EPS, since it was never mentioned on the call despite core EPS being the headline number throughout.

## Customer Deposits (Optical/company-wide LTA financing mechanism)

- Total customer deposits: ~$1.3B (3/31/2026) vs ~$1.5B (12/31/2025) — $183M current + $1,136M non-current
- Description: "non-refundable and allowed customers to secure rights to products produced by Corning under long-term supply agreements, generally over a period of up to 10 years"
- Recognized into revenue in Q1 2026: $186M (vs $62M in Q1 2025 — roughly 3x YoY)
- **Not broken out by segment** — the 10-Q explicitly aggregates this company-wide, meaning the balance cannot be isolated to the new Meta/hyperscaler Optical LTAs versus older long-term agreements elsewhere (e.g., the Gen 10.5 display agreements Wendell himself cited as the template for the new optical deals).

## Deferred Revenue (Solar/Hemlock-specific mechanism, separate from customer deposits)

- Total deferred revenue: ~$737M (3/31/2026) vs ~$775M (12/31/2025) — $231M current + $506M non-current
- "Primarily related to the performance obligations of non-refundable consideration previously received by Hemlock Semiconductor Group... from its customers under long-term supply agreements"
- Recognized in Q1 2026: $45M (vs $39M in Q1 2025)
- Recognition method: per-unit amount recognized as customers take delivery of committed volumes

## Solar / Government Incentives (Section 48D credits)

- Q1 2026: recognized $149M of right-of-use assets/lease liabilities (finance leases, 5-8 year terms) for solar equipment at the Hemlock, Michigan facility; $49M of Section 48D Advanced Manufacturing Investment Credits (renamed under "the One Big Beautiful Bill Act") recognized, reducing the carrying value of those ROU assets
- Leases not yet commenced: ~$413M (undiscounted), expected to commence in 2026, 5-16 year terms, expected ~$80M of additional 48D credits once placed in service
- Government incentives on balance sheet: current $452M (up sharply from $163M at YE2025), non-current $134M (down from $330M) — the jump in the current portion suggests incentive recognition timing shifted toward near-term
- Non-controlling interest primarily represents Hemlock Semiconductor Operations (majority-owned, Solar segment) and US Conec (a VIE within Optical Communications, Corning is primary beneficiary)

## Tax Matters (both unmentioned on the earnings call)

- **South Korea**: Feb 11, 2026 final unfavorable ruling on Corning Precision Materials tax dispute for tax years 2010-2012 → **$92M noncash charge to the Q1 2026 income tax provision** (reduced a receivable balance). Remaining tax years 2013-2019 still under appeal; company still believes "more likely than not" to prevail on those.
- **Luxembourg**: March 12, 2026 final unfavorable ruling on tax years 2015-2018 → reduced deferred tax assets (NOL carryforwards) by $879M, offset by an equal reduction in the related valuation allowance — explicitly **no net income tax provision impact** ("no impact on the Company's income tax provision"), but a large gross balance-sheet remeasurement in the same quarter.
- IRS is examining US federal returns for 2015-2018 and 2019-2020 (including the TCJA one-time transition tax); Corning believes "more likely than not" to sustain its position.
