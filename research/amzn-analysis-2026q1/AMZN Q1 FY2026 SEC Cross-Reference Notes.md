## OpenAI investment — undisclosed on the earnings call (10-Q Note 3 / MD&A)

In Q1 2026, Amazon:
1. Invested **$15.0 billion** in OpenAI's Series C Preferred Stock.
2. Signed an **Equity Commitment Letter Agreement** to purchase an additional **$35.0 billion** of Series C Preferred Stock (the "Commitment Shares") — total potential exposure **$50.0B** — exercisable at Amazon's discretion, but mandatory upon OpenAI hitting specified milestones or an IPO/direct listing, with a hard deadline of **December 31, 2028**.
3. Entered a **commercial arrangement** for AWS cloud services to an OpenAI affiliate — explicitly **including the use of AWS chips (Trainium)** — plus a **joint collaboration agreement** to make OpenAI models available on AWS/Bedrock.
4. Combined Anthropic + OpenAI equity-investment carrying value: **$16.2B (Dec 2025) → $48.1B (Mar 2026)**.

**⚑ Silence-as-signal:** None of the $15B/$35B OpenAI equity figures were mentioned on the call — Jassy referenced only the *product* integration ("we're excited to make OpenAI's models available in Bedrock," GPT‑5.4 launch). The 10-Q reveals Amazon simultaneously (a) taking a ~$50B combined equity swing position across the two frontier labs it also sells compute to, and (b) locking in an AWS-chips supply commitment to OpenAI — a customer that is also becoming a Trainium consumer, deepening the circularity between Amazon's chip business, its cloud backlog, and its own balance-sheet bets on the labs generating that backlog.

## Anthropic — full note detail

- Cumulative **$8.0B** invested in Anthropic convertible notes from Q3 2023 through Q4 2025 (available-for-sale, fair value through OCI).
- Portions converted to **nonvoting preferred stock** in Q1 2025 *and* Q1 2026 — each conversion triggers a reclassification of the note's accumulated unrealized OCI gain into "Other income (expense), net": **$3.3B** (Q1'25) and **$4.5B** (Q1'26).
- Q1 2026 also recorded a further **$12.3B upward fair-value adjustment** to the nonvoting preferred stock itself (observable price change).
- Nonvoting preferred stock balance-sheet carrying value: **$14.8B (Dec 2025) → $32.0B (Mar 2026)**.
- The "$100 billion Anthropic deal" Jassy referenced on the call (re: AWS backlog breadth) is a **separate compute/commercial commitment**, not part of this equity note — it postdates the quarter and is not yet reflected in the 10-Q's numbers.

## Globalstar acquisition — subsequent event (10-Q Note 8)

- **April 13, 2026**: definitive merger agreement, cash-and-stock.
- Consideration: **$90.00/share cash** or **0.3210 AMZN shares** (capped at $90.00/share value) at each holder's election.
- **Cash elections capped at 40%** of total Globalstar shares; excess cash elections auto-convert to stock pro rata.
- Downward price adjustment of **up to $110M** if Globalstar misses satellite-replacement operational milestones.
- Implied deal value **≈$10.9B including debt** as of the agreement date.
- **Apple Inc.**, Globalstar's largest customer, simultaneously signed agreements to (a) continue receiving services post-close and (b) **redeem its existing equity interest in a Globalstar special-purpose entity** — clarifying that Apple was already a Globalstar equity stakeholder before the Amazon Leo tie-up, not a fresh counterparty.
- **Expected close: 2027** — later than the "commercial service in a few months" framing on the call might imply; that refers to Amazon Leo's own constellation service, which is independent of (and ahead of) the Globalstar deal closing.

## Supply chain disclosure — lower confidence, generic language

Unlike a semiconductor-manufacturer 10-K, Amazon's FY2025 10-K Risk Factors contain only generic supplier language: *"we rely on a limited group of suppliers for semiconductor products, including products related to artificial intelligence infrastructure such as graphics processing units. Constraints on the availability of these products could adversely affect our ability to develop and operate artificial intelligence technologies..."* No named foundries, memory vendors, or customer-concentration percentages are disclosed (Amazon does not name TSMC, SK Hynix/Samsung/Micron, or any AI-lab customer by name in risk factors). ⚑ **Confidence flag: LOW** for any supply-chain node beyond what management stated on the call or in the Anthropic/OpenAI notes above — the 10-K's silence here is itself informative (Amazon does not carry the single-customer/single-supplier concentration disclosures that, e.g., a foundry or memory maker would).
